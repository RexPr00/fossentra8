Warum dieser Markt jetzt

Compute scarcity as pricing power

Persistent accelerator constraints convert quality GPU capacity exposure into a strategic bottleneck asset with measurable pricing discipline.

Enterprise AI workloads are sticky

Production AI systems depend on predictable low-latency clusters, supporting recurring enterprise demand growth and lower churn risk.

Power-first deployment economics

Grid access and power contracts define who can scale; institutional compute allocation must model energy procurement deeply.

Infrastructure yield logic over hype

Returns should be tied to contracted utilization, uptime, and renewal covenants, not only valuation multiples.

Sovereign digital policy tailwinds

National compute resilience agendas are expanding demand for domestic and allied AI capacity infrastructure.

Secondary market optionality

Modular clusters and transferable contracts create optional exits and refinancing pathways in a risk-managed infrastructure asset framework.
